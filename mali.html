Mali - Africa

Country Information

The Republic of Mali or Mali is a landlocked 3rd world country located in Northwestern Africa. In 2017 the population was recorded at 18,541,980 people which was a 3.04% increase from the 2016 population (17,994,837). It is predicted that by 2050 the population will have increased by 18.74% to 44,020,171 people, in turn creating an average of 36 people per 1km2 (worldometers, 2018). Mali has a total land area of 1,240,192km2 and nearly all this land mass is made up of the desert and grasslands (known in Africa as a savanna) biomes, much of which has been greatly cultivated for agricultural purposes. However, Mali’s land is continuing to be affected by climate change with great increases in temperature and great decreases in rainfall. Due to this, desertification has started turning much of their fertile land into dry desert grounds (wfp, 2018). This is a major issue as 90% of Mali’s population is employed in the farming and agriculture industry and these farms are the main supply of their staple foods.

Food Security Issues

Their staple foods largely consist of carbohydrates and poultry such as rice, millet and sorghum and chicken and fish (WTG, 2018). These ingredients are very popular in Malian meals as they are readily available and easy and cheap to produce. However, these staple foods are not nourishing and most likely don’t provide the people with enough nutrients to live an active and healthy lifestyle. Most of their agriculture and farming practices such as harvesting, and fishing take place along the Niger River as it is where the most, and only fertile soil is. However, the Niger River only flows through central and southern Mali leaving out the north. This means cities like Taoudenni, Bordj-Mokhtar, and Araouane which are secluded from the rest, do not have good soil and land for their farming.
