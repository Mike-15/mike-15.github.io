WW1 History

Brief History of the Great War

The Great War broke out due to the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinard of Austria-Hungary. They all expected it to last only 6 months, however soldiers who inlisted in this war found themselves fighting at the fornt line of battles, living in trenches and surviving off rations for 4 years. Nevertheless, as they were equipped with the right gear, the brave soldiers of World War 1 (WW1) were able to continue on. This museum exhibit outlines the equipment that was apart of an Australian Soldier's uniform, the different kit required for certain occasions and the change of this equipment through the 4 years of conflict.


This official equipment took form in two setups, battle and marching & trench arrangment. These two configurations were very similar both containing:

When the war first started it was declared between Serbia and Austria-Hungary, by the the end there was over 30 countries involved. However, throughout the entire conflict there were 2 main sides, The Allies or Triple Entente and The Triple Alliance. The Allies comprised of Britain, France and Russia. The Triple Alliance embodied Germany, Austria-Hungary and Italy. Other countries such as Australia, New Zealand and Canada joined the war to help Britain.



Timeline of the Lead up to War

(hover on date to view event)

  1. 28 June 1914

    Archduke Franz Ferdinard is assassinated.

  2. 5 July 1914

    Germany promises for help to Austria-Hungary.

  3. 23 July 1914

    Austria-Hungary presents a warning to Serbia.

  4. 28 July 1914

    Austria-Hungary declares war on Serbia.

  5. 30 July 1914

    Russia mobilises their troops to support Serbia.

  6. 1 August 1914

    Germany declares war on Russia.

  7. 3 August 1914

    Germany declares war on France and then invades Belgium.

  8. 4 August 1914

    Britain declares war on Germany and Australia and New Zealand join war to help Britain.

  9. 6 August 1914

    Austria-Hungary declares war on Russia and Serbia declares war on Germany.

  10. 12 August 1914

    Britain and France declare war on Austria-Hungary.

  11. 11 November 1918

    WW1 Ends with the Triple Entente in victory.
