Food Security

A country is classified as being food secure when they have reliable access to a suffient amount of safe and nutritious food. This food should allow a person to have an active and healthly lifestyle and should meet all dietary requirements and food preferences.

Those countries facing or with food insecurity can have many issues with their food including:

    • the affordability
    • the availability
    • the quality and
    • the safety

Biomes

Biomes are large, distinct regions that share similar landscapes, climates and vegetation. The world is split up into 8 different biomes which are classified as:

    • Polar Lands
    • Tundra
    • Boreal Forest
    • Mountain Vegetation
    • Temperate Forest
    • Grassland (called 'Prairies' in USA, 'Savannas' in Africa and 'Steppes' in Asia)
    • Desert and
    • Tropical Forest

Climates

Climate classifies the particular trend of weather and temperature a distinct region has over a long period of time. Climate is a major influencer in the type of farming a country or location practices due to the average temperature and rainfall. These climates are:

    • Polar
    • Cold Wet
    • Cold Dry Winter
    • Cold Semi Deset
    • Cold Desert
    • Mild Wet
    • Highlands
    • Subtropical Wet
    • Subtropical Dry Winter
    • Hot Semi Desert
    • Tropical Wet and Dry and
    • Tropical Wet

Food Insecurity in Our World

Most food insecure places are in regions with extreme climates, for example very hot and dry (arid) or very cold and wet (polar), because the average rainfall and temperatures there are not suitable for normal farming practices. As well, countries that’s main biome/s yield only unusable and inadequate land for agriculture and farming purposes can make a country incapable of growing the things they need to sustain and feed a whole population.

Some examples of this are in Haiti - North America, Mali - Africa and Laos - Southeast Asia. These countries have not reached food security as it is difficult for them to produce and provide ample food due to natural disasters, barren land and climate change. Along with economic and political reasons, these three factors are very common contributors to food insecurity in countries all over the world.
