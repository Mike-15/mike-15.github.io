Levers and Balls

"Imagine a series of wheels which that is shown above is one. One lever is in the act of falling from the periphery of the wheel into he right line.
Note that the lever is compossed of a series of jointed flat rods, provided with a stop to prevent their collapsing at any time more than enough to bring any one of the rods at a right angle with the rod next to it.
The lever is hinged to the periphrey of the wheel in such a way as to prevent its falling into any other than a right line from the center of the circumference of the wheel.
Each lever has a bucket at its outer extremity, the bottom being suffiently broad to retain a ball. The balls a supposed to roll out upon the inclined plane and roll to the other end, ready to be taken again into the buckets." (Popular Science, 1920, p. 27)
