Thermodynamics

"The branch of physics which deals with the energy and work of a system.”(Britannica, ND)

As the name suggests, a perpetual motion machine is something, a machine, that has ongoing and uninterrupted movement (Merriam-Webster, ND). The idea of creating such a machine originated in 1159 A.D. from a mathematician named Bhaskara the Learned. He drew one of the first ideas for an unbalanced wheel but instead of marbles it contained mercury. He reasoned that “as the wheel spun, the mercury would flow onto one side of the wheel making it heavier than the other side causing it to spin forever” (Ted-Ed, 2017).

A true perpetual motion machine would need to give out more energy than what is put in or required to run it. This would mean creating enough energy to overcome friction and air resistance, enough to keep it going and going until the machine itself breaks (Scott, 2016). This may seem easy, like we’ve done it before, wind turbines and solar panels, but there are limitations to what makes a perpetual motion machine a true perpetual motion machine.

Key Points

  • A perpetual motion machine is something that has ongoing and uninterrupted movement.
  • A true perpetual motion machine would need to give out more enrgy than what is put in.
  • There are limitations to what makes a true perpetual motion machine.
