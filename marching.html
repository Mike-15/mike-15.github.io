Marching & Trench Equipment

Equipment used by Australian soldiers in the Trenches

Trench Coat

Issued: 1914 (Start of War)

All the items in marching & trench arrangment were stored in a large removeable pack.


The trench coat was a big shoulder to ankle cotton coat. This was worn at night time in the trenches and when soldiers were being transported. Some version has removeable insulation for warmer months.

Mess Kit

Issued: 1914 (Start of War)

Thid was just 2 little tins that soldiers used to eat and drink out of. For this reason they had to keep them really clean. However they were commonly stored outside the pack leading to them getting dirty even though it had a cover.

Toiletries

Issued: 1914 (Start of War)

A soldier's toiletries included a tooth brush, a shaving brush, a razor blade, a comb, a towel and some soap.

Housewife

Issued: 1914 (Start of War)

A house wife was a small sewing kit with some thread and needles. It was named 'Housewife' as the women were usally found doing the sewing. This was used to mend the uniform.

Extra Spare Clothing

Issued: 1914 (Start of War)

They just had some more spare clothing because they spent more time living in the trenches than out of them.

Paybook

Issued: 1914 (Start of War)

A paybook was all of a soldiers document. It had his personal information including vaccinations, eye percription, date of birth and the battalion he was apart of. There was also his work information for pay, will and next of kin. The paybook itself was a brown labelled pouch to hold all of that information.
