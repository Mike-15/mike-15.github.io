Law 1

“Energy can neither be created nor destroyed.”(Lumen Learning, ND)

The first law of thermodynamics states that “energy can neither be created nor destroyed” (Lumen Learning, ND). This law is also defined as the Law of Conservation of Energy and means that energy can only be transferred and transformed from one form to another. In relation to perpetual motion machines, this explains what happens to all the energy that is lost whilst the machine is running and as well as why it is impossible for the machines to be 100% efficient.

When considering a type of perpetual motion machine called an unbalanced wheel, there are two distinct areas where energy is lost through transformations and transfers. The first loss is through the centre axle. Whenever there are two parts rubbing together there will always be a loss from kinetic energy transforming into heat energy due to friction. No matter how smooth the two surfaces are or how little they are moving against each other, there will be friction, a loss of energy lowering the efficiency. The second loss is through the rolling marbles. Every sound, touch and movement is a sign of energy being lost, and when it comes to the marbles of an unbalanced wheel there are no exceptions. There is friction between the track and marble whilst it is rolling and there is a transformation of kinetic energy (into sound energy) when the marble hits the wall. All these transformations and transfers may be minuscul but because they exist, it has an effect. These minute loses may not lower the efficiency very much, but they are reason perpetual motion machines cannot run forever.
The efficiency of a machine, engine or anything that uses energy, is equal to the energy out divided by the energy in, multiplied by 100.

Key Points

  • Energy can only be transferred and transformed from one form to another.
  • It is impossible for perpetual motion machines to be 100% efficient.
  • Friction is the main cause for loss of energy in perpetual motion machines.
