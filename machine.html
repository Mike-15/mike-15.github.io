Machine Examples

Included here are a range of supposed perpetual motion machines that featured in the 1920 October issue of Popular Science. All descriptions and images of the machines are taken directly from the magazine.
Theoretically all of these machines should work as intended but they don't. The short explanation for this is physics doesn't let them. Looking at it on a serious note though, it is becasue the first and second laws of thermodynamics prevent them from living up to their true name. A suposed perpetual motion machine might look like its working or work for a little bit, but even then if it is doing any of the two things above, it is not actually running off perpetual motion.
