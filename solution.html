Green House Solution - Idea Proposal

One proposal that would focus on improving the quality and quantity of food in Mali is to create a system or network of sustainable greenhouses around the countries main cities and villages. Not only would this be a cheap and effective solution, but it would mean Malians would be able to grow a wider range of more nutritious and nourishing foods which would improve their health and wellbeing. This would also provide a new range of jobs other than a farmer as the greenhouses would need to be maintained in several different ways.

The biggest problem Mali is having in reaching food security is the rising temperatures from climate change. This is causing their land to become unusable and there to be less water available for irrigation. Greenhouses, however, rely on their own source of soil and water instead of what the environment and land provide. This means that food could be grown all over the country even in the desert and arid regions.

Green House Solution - Logistics

The only restriction involved is that there would need to be a readily available source of water to irrigate the crops whether it be a river, or a water tank and the soil might have to be re-fertilised or replaced when needed, to keep the crop growth strong and healthy. There should be no problems with these limitations as they would have already been a factor in their original farming practices.

With this, it should be noted that this would probably not work as a long-term solution. Eventually, the restrictions with the water and soil would become a bigger problem and the greenhouses might not stay as standing structures forever, knowing they will have to be built on a budget. However, in other countries with more money, this would work just as well because they would have more resources and time to put into the greenhouses. With Mali though they would want to get it done in as little time as possible, so their situation can be fixed and a thing of the past.
